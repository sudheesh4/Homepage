Readings

Here are some curious recent resources I find insightful and interesting:

  • Wave Turbulence and Energy Cascade in the Hippocampus

    Mesoscale cortical activity refers to the coordination of large groups of neurons, resulting in patterns like traveling waves. Understanding this collective behavior is important for grasping brain activity's cross-scale integration and cognitive emergence. To address this, they analyzed hippocampal recordings, finding that activity dynamics become stronger and more nonlinear with increased energy input. Their findings suggest that these dynamics follow weak turbulence principles, indicating stochastic behavior and weak dissipation.They propose weak turbulence as a framework for mesoscopic hippocampal activity, providing insights into energy exchange and phenomena like theta-gamma coupling. This highlights the ubiqutous-ness of turbulent cascades as mechanisms of optimal cross-scale integration and information transfer in nature.

  • A Mathematical perspective on Transformers

    This paper delves into the mathematical underpinnings of Transformer neural network architectures, particularly focusing on their representation as flow maps on probability measure spaces. It outlines a simplified model capturing key Transformer components like self-attention and layer-normalization, analyzing their behavior through clustering and posing open research questions. Additionally, it explores the infinite-width limit of Transformers, discussing their approximation properties and training dynamics, while proposing a surrogate model for their dynamics on the unit sphere, described as a Wasserstein gradient flow for interaction energy. This perspective suggests that action of a transformer (during inference or training) can be viewed as an abstract mathematical object, formally called vector bundle. These bundles are a central concept in differential geometry and physics. Such parallels are curious and may help uncover the black-box-ness of neural networks.

  • On bubble rings and ink chandeliers

    This paper introduces a novel model for simulating variable thickness vortex filaments in viscous fluids, offering insights into phenomena like bubble rings and ink chandeliers. By decoupling linear and rotational energy components and considering dissipative drag and buoyancy, the model accurately captures the intricate dynamics of these filaments. Utilizing equations derived from Newtonian dynamics and geodesic motion on a Riemannian manifold, the study elucidates the evolution of filament geometry, curvature, and circulation. Through numerical simulations based on the Cauchy formulation of Euler equations, the model's efficacy is demonstrated, aligning well with experimental observations. This differential geometry ('bundle' based) approach suggests that the 'behaviour' of vortex rings (specifically their thickness) has similar behaviour as Buurger's equation, which can also be viewed as a general class of interface growth patterns[1]. A demo of the said 'ink-chandeliers' can be found HERE.

    [1]Mehran Kardar, et al, Phys. Rev. Lett. 56, 889

  • Probing Hydrodynamic Fluctuation-Induced Forces with an Oscillating Robot

    A novel robot demonstrates the ability to navigate through water by generating surface waves and interacting with nearby boundaries. When generating monochromatic waves, the robot exploits the asymmetry of the wave field caused by reflection from a boundary to propel itself forward. This propulsion mechanism relies on fluctuation-induced forces within the wave field, where the distance between the robot and the boundary determines the strength and direction of the force. The interaction between the robot's vibrations and the surrounding fluid produces surface currents, whose magnitude varies with the vibration frequency. A simplified force model predicts the robot's motion in response to attraction, offering insights into its dynamics near boundaries. This study suggests the existence of transition dynamics between symmetric and asymmetric regimes, indicating potential applications in controlled navigation.

  • Arousal as a universal embedding for spatiotemporal brain dynamics

    Arousal, the state of being awake and alert, is influenced by various factors like the sleep-wake cycle and stress. This study explored how arousal impacts brain activity in awake mice. It was found that arousal plays a crucial role in organizing brain function, as it can accurately predict changes in brain dynamics over time. By measuring pupil size or using a single principal component, they were able to reconstruct complex brain activity patterns, suggesting that arousal serves as a key organizing principle for brain function. This framework offers insights into how arousal dynamics synchronize with brain activity, impacting physiological processes across the brain. Furthermore, it suggests a unified approach to interpreting diverse physiological measurements, offering a deeper understanding of the relationship between arousal and brain function. The ingenious use of dynamical systems and encoder-decoder architecture to unify and find a common 'embedding' ground for mutimodal sense-data in organism can be insightful for understading not only human cognition but also possibly inform design of novel neural network architectures.
